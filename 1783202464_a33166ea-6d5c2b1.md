<p>Testing markdown:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Bold</strong>: <code>**text**</code> or <code>__text__</code> becomes <strong>text</strong>.</li>
<li><em>Italics</em>: <code>*text*</code> or <code>_text_</code> becomes <em>text</em>.</li>
<li><em><strong>Bold and Italic</strong></em>: <code>***text***</code> becomes <em><strong>text</strong></em>.</li>
<li>~~Strikethrough~~: <code>~~text~~</code> becomes ~~text~~.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Ordered List</strong>: Use numbers followed by a period.</p>
<ol>
<li>First item</li>
<li>Second item</li>
</ol>
<p><strong>Task List</strong>: Use square brackets.</p>
<ul>
<li>[x] Completed task</li>
<li>[ ] Incomplete task</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Blockquotes</strong>: Use the <code>&gt;</code> character.</p>
<blockquote>
<p>This is a blockquote for nested text or quotes.</p>
</blockquote>
<p><strong>Tables</strong>: Use pipes <code>|</code> and hyphens <code>-</code> to create columns and rows.</p>
<p>| Column 1 | Column 2 |
| :--- | :--- |
| Cell A | Cell B |</p>
<p><strong>Horizontal Rule</strong>: Three or more asterisks (<code>***</code>) or hyphens (<code>---</code>)
create a divider line:</p>
<hr>
<p><strong>Escaping Characters</strong>: Use a backslash <code>\</code> to show a literal character
instead of its Markdown formatting.</p>
